## 1. Sole Proprietorship Registration (ACRA)

### What It Is
A Sole Proprietorship is the simplest business structure in Singapore. The DP holder (wife) will be the registered owner.

### Prerequisites
- Valid Dependent Pass (DP)
- SingPass account (DP holders can register for SingPass)
- Local residential address

### Step-by-Step Procedure

**Step 1: Log in to BizFile+**
- Go to [https://www.bizfile.gov.sg](https://www.bizfile.gov.sg)
- Click "Log in" → Select "SingPass"
- Enter SingPass credentials and complete 2FA verification

**Step 2: Start New Application**
- From dashboard, click "Start a New Business"
- Select "Sole Proprietorship" as business structure

**Step 3: Fill in Business Details**
| Field | What to Enter |
|-------|---------------|
| Business Name | e.g., "SG Cubing Co." (check availability first) |
| Principal Activity | "Retail sale of games and toys" (SSIC Code: 47620) |
| Secondary Activity | "Retail sale via internet" (SSIC Code: 47910) |
| Business Address | Home address (HDB flat address) |
| Start Date | Date you want business to start |

**Step 4: Fill in Owner Details**
- Full name (as per DP)
- NRIC/FIN number
- Date of birth
- Nationality
- Residential address

**Step 5: Declare No Disqualifications**
- Confirm you are not undischarged bankrupt
- Confirm you are not disqualified from managing a business

**Step 6: Review & Submit**
- Review all information carefully
- Tick the declaration box
- Click "Submit"

**Step 7: Make Payment**
- Fee: **$100 SGD** (1-year registration)
- Payment methods: Credit/Debit card, PayNow
- Payment is processed immediately

**Step 8: Receive Confirmation**
- Download the **Certificate of Registration** immediately
- Also download the **Business Profile** (free for first 30 days)
- Save both documents as PDF

### Documents to Keep
- [ ] Certificate of Registration (PDF)
- [ ] Business Profile (PDF)
- [ ] Payment receipt

### Important Notes
- Registration is valid for **1 year** — must renew annually ($30 SGD)
- Must update ACRA within 14 days if any details change
- Business name reservation is optional ($15 SGD) but recommended

## 2. Letter of Consent (LOC) — MOM

### What It Is
A Letter of Consent from the Ministry of Manpower allows a Dependent Pass holder to start and operate a business in Singapore.

### Prerequisites
- Valid Dependent Pass (DP)
- Registered Sole Proprietorship (from Step 1)
- Employer sponsor (the business entity itself)

### Step-by-Step Procedure

**Step 1: Log in to EP Online**
- Go to [https://www.mom.gov.sg/employment-practices/employment-pass-online](https://www.mom.gov.sg/employment-practices/employment-pass-online)
- Click "Log in" → Select "SingPass"

**Step 2: Start LOC Application**
- From dashboard, select "Apply for LOC"
- Select "For Dependent Pass holder starting own business"

**Step 3: Fill in Application Details**

| Field | What to Enter |
|-------|---------------|
| Applicant Details | Auto-filled from SingPass |
| Business Name | "SG Cubing Co." (as registered with ACRA) |
| UEN | Will be auto-assigned (see Step 4) |
| Job Title | "Owner / Sole Proprietor" |
| Monthly Salary | $0 (owner, not employee) |
| Job Description | "Managing online retail business for speedcubes and accessories" |

**Step 4: Upload Supporting Documents**
- Copy of Dependent Pass (front & back)
- Certificate of Registration from ACRA
- Business Profile from ACRA
- Passport biodata page
- Educational certificates (highest qualification)

**Step 5: Submit Application**
- Review all details
- Tick declaration boxes
- Click "Submit"

**Step 6: Wait for Processing**
- Processing time: **3-5 weeks**
- MOM may request additional documents
- Check status regularly on EP Online

**Step 7: Receive Outcome**
- If approved: LOC letter will be available for download
- If rejected: MOM will state reasons; can appeal within 30 days

**Step 8: Download & Save LOC**
- Download the LOC letter (PDF)
- Print a copy for physical records

### Documents to Keep
- [ ] Letter of Consent (PDF + printed copy)
- [ ] Application confirmation receipt
- [ ] Any correspondence from MOM

### Important Notes
- LOC is tied to the DP — if DP expires/renews, LOC must be re-applied
- LOC allows you to **own and operate** the business
- Without LOC, operating a business on DP is **illegal** and may result in pass cancellation
- Processing may take longer during peak periods

## 3. Business Bank Account

### What It Is
A dedicated bank account for business transactions, separate from personal finances.

### Prerequisites
- Certificate of Registration (ACRA)
- Business Profile (ACRA)
- Letter of Consent (MOM)
- Valid DP and passport
- SingPass account

### Recommended Banks

| Bank | Account Type | Monthly Fee | Min Balance |
|------|-------------|-------------|-------------|
| **DBS** | Business Digital Account | $0 | $1,000 |
| **OCBC** | Business Growth Account | $0 | $1,000 |
| **UOB** | eBusiness Account | $0 | $1,000 |

### Step-by-Step Procedure

**Step 1: Choose a Bank**
- DBS is recommended (largest network, best digital banking)
- Visit bank website or branch

**Step 2: Book an Appointment**
- DBS: [https://www.dbs.com.sg/corporate](https://www.dbs.com.sg/corporate)
- Select "Open a Business Account"
- Choose "Sole Proprietorship"
- Book appointment at preferred branch

**Step 3: Prepare Documents for Appointment**
| Document | Copies |
|----------|--------|
| Certificate of Registration (ACRA) | 1 |
| Business Profile (ACRA) | 1 |
| Letter of Consent (MOM) | 1 |
| Passport | Original + 1 copy |
| Dependent Pass | Original + 1 copy |
| Proof of residential address | 1 (utility bill or bank statement) |
| Completed application form | Download from bank website |

**Step 4: Attend Appointment**
- Bring all documents
- Bank officer will verify documents
- Sign account opening forms
- Set up online banking access

**Step 5: Receive Account Details**
- Account number issued immediately
- Debit card mailed within 7-10 days
- Online banking activated within 1-2 days

**Step 6: Set Up Payment Gateway**
- Link business bank account to Shopify Payments or Stripe
- Set up PayNow for business (optional, 0% fees)

### Documents to Keep
- [ ] Account opening confirmation
- [ ] Debit card details (securely stored)
- [ ] Online banking credentials (securely stored)

### Important Notes
- Minimum balance requirement: ~$1,000 SGD (below this, monthly fee applies)
- Keep business and personal finances completely separate
- Set up automatic transfers for GST payments (when applicable)

## 4. CorpPass + UEN Setup

### What It Is
- **UEN (Unique Entity Number):** A standard identification number for businesses in Singapore (like a business NRIC)
- **CorpPass:** A corporate digital identity for businesses to transact with government agencies

### Prerequisites
- Registered Sole Proprietorship (ACRA)
- SingPass account

### Step-by-Step Procedure

**Step 1: Receive UEN Automatically**
- UEN is automatically assigned when you register with ACRA
- Found on your Certificate of Registration
- Format: e.g., "53123456A"

**Step 2: Set Up CorpPass Admin Account**
- Go to [https://www.corppass.gov.sg](https://www.corppass.gov.sg)
- Click "Register as CorpPass Admin"
- Log in with SingPass

**Step 3: Register Your Business**
- Enter UEN
- Verify business details (auto-populated from ACRA)
- Confirm admin details

**Step 4: Create CorpPass Accounts**
- Set up **Admin Account** (yourself as DP holder)
- Set up **User Account** (if you have helpers, optional initially)
- Assign roles and permissions

**Step 5: Link Government Services**
- Link to **IRAS** (for tax filing)
- Link to **Customs** (for import permits)
- Link to **ACRA** (for annual filings)

**Step 6: Download CorpPass**
- Download CorpPass mobile app (optional)
- Save login credentials securely

### Documents to Keep
- [ ] UEN number (on Certificate of Registration)
- [ ] CorpPass login credentials
- [ ] CorpPass admin confirmation

### Important Notes
- UEN never changes — use this number for all government interactions
- CorpPass is needed for filing taxes, applying for permits, etc.
- Keep CorpPass credentials secure — it's your digital business identity

## 5. GST Registration (IRAS)

### What It Is
Goods and Services Tax (GST) registration is required when annual turnover exceeds $1 million SGD.

### When to Register
- **Mandatory:** When taxable turnover exceeds $1 million in past 12 months
- **Voluntary:** Can register early if beneficial (usually not for small businesses)

### For SG Cubing Co.
- **Not needed initially** (Year 1 revenue projection: ~$21,000)
- Monitor turnover quarterly
- Register when approaching $800,000 threshold (to allow buffer)

### Step-by-Step Procedure (When Needed)

**Step 1: Log in to myTax Portal**
- Go to [https://mytax.iras.gov.sg](https://mytax.iras.gov.sg)
- Log in with CorpPass

**Step 2: Apply for GST Registration**
- Navigate to "Register for GST"
- Select "Compulsory GST Registration"

**Step 3: Provide Business Details**
- UEN
- Business name
- Date of liability (when turnover crossed $1M)
- Bank account details

**Step 4: Submit Supporting Documents**
- Financial statements or management accounts
- Sales records showing turnover exceeds $1M
- Business Profile from ACRA

**Step 5: Await Approval**
- Processing time: ~10 working days
- IRAS may request additional information

**Step 6: Receive GST Registration**
- GST registration number issued
- Must display GST number on invoices
- Must charge 9% GST on all sales

### Documents to Keep
- [ ] GST registration certificate
- [ ] GST registration number
- [ ] IRAS correspondence

### Important Notes
- Once registered, must file GST returns quarterly
- Must charge 9% GST on all taxable supplies
- Can claim input GST on business expenses
- Late registration penalty: 10% of GST due + $200 fine

## 6. PDPA Compliance

### What It Is
The Personal Data Protection Act (PDPA) requires businesses to protect customer personal data.

### Prerequisites
- Registered business
- Website/app collecting customer data

### Step-by-Step Procedure

**Step 1: Understand PDPA Requirements**
- Must have a **Privacy Policy** on website
- Must obtain **consent** before collecting data
- Must allow customers to **access and correct** their data
- Must **protect** data from unauthorized access
- Must **retain** data only as long as necessary

**Step 2: Create Privacy Policy**
Your privacy policy must include:
- What personal data you collect (name, email, address, phone)
- Why you collect it (order fulfillment, marketing)
- How you use it
- Who you share it with (logistics partners, payment processors)
- How customers can access/correct their data
- How to contact you for data-related queries

**Step 3: Add Privacy Policy to Website**
- Create a dedicated "Privacy Policy" page on Shopify
- Link it in the website footer
- Link it during checkout (checkbox for consent)

**Step 4: Implement Consent Mechanisms**
- Add checkbox at checkout: "I agree to the Privacy Policy"
- Add checkbox for marketing emails: "Subscribe to newsletter"
- Record consent (Shopify does this automatically)

**Step 5: Set Up Data Access Process**
- Create email address for data requests: privacy@sgcubing.com
- Process requests within 30 days
- Keep log of all data requests

**Step 6: Implement Security Measures**
- Use HTTPS (Shopify provides this)
- Use strong passwords
- Limit data access to authorized persons only
- Regularly update software

### Sample Privacy Policy Template

```
PRIVACY POLICY — SG Cubing Co.
Last Updated: [Date]

1. INFORMATION WE COLLECT
We collect your name, email address, shipping address, phone number, and payment information when you place an order.

2. HOW WE USE YOUR INFORMATION
We use your information to process orders, send order updates, and (with your consent) send marketing communications.

3. INFORMATION SHARING
We share your information with logistics partners (Ninja Van, J&T) for delivery and payment processors for payment processing.

4. YOUR RIGHTS
You may access, correct, or request deletion of your personal data by contacting us at privacy@sgcubing.com.

5. DATA RETENTION
We retain your data for 3 years after your last order for warranty and legal purposes.

6. CONTACT US
For privacy inquiries, contact: privacy@sgcubing.com
```

### Documents to Keep
- [ ] Privacy Policy (published on website)
- [ ] Consent mechanism screenshots
- [ ] Data access request log template

### Important Notes
- PDPA applies to all businesses collecting personal data
- Non-compliance penalties: up to $1 million SGD or 10% of annual turnover
- Review privacy policy annually
- Train anyone handling customer data on PDPA requirements

## 7. Import Permit (Singapore Customs)

### What It Is
A permit required for importing goods into Singapore when shipment value exceeds $400 SGD.

### When Required
- Shipment value **> $400 SGD**
- **0% import duty on toys** (no duty to pay, just permit)

### Prerequisites
- Registered business with UEN
- CorpPass account linked to Customs

### Step-by-Step Procedure

**Step 1: Register as Importer**
- Go to [https://www.trade.gov.sg](https://www.trade.gov.sg) (TradeNet)
- Log in with CorpPass
- Register as importer

**Step 2: Apply for Customs Account**
- Submit application on TradeNet
- Provide UEN and business details
- Processing: 1-2 working days

**Step 3: Determine HS Code for Products**
- Speedcubes fall under: **9503.00** (Toys, games, and sports requisites)
- This code has **0% import duty**

**Step 4: Prepare Import Documents**
| Document | Source |
|----------|--------|
| Commercial Invoice | From XingleToy |
| Packing List | From XingleToy |
| Bill of Lading / Airway Bill | From freight forwarder |
| Certificate of Origin (if applicable) | From supplier |

**Step 5: Submit Import Declaration**
- Log in to TradeNet
- Select "Import Declaration"
- Enter shipment details:
  - Supplier name and address
  - HS Code: 9503.00
  - Value of goods
  - Quantity and description
- Upload supporting documents

**Step 6: Make Payment**
- **No duty** (0% on toys)
- GST on import: **9% of CIF value** (can be claimed back if GST-registered)
- Declaration fee: ~$2-8 SGD per declaration

**Step 7: Receive Permit**
- Approval usually within minutes (electronic)
- Download permit letter
- Forward to freight forwarder for customs clearance

**Step 8: Customs Clearance**
- Freight forwarder presents permit to Singapore Customs
- Goods released
- Arrange delivery to your address

### Using a Customs Broker (Recommended)

For simplicity, you can use a **freight forwarder** who handles customs clearance:
- They submit the import declaration on your behalf
- They handle all documentation
- They deliver goods to your door
- Additional cost: ~$20-50 SGD per shipment

**Recommended Freight Forwarders:**
- Janio (e-commerce focused)
- DHL eCommerce
- Ninja Direct (same company as Ninja Van)

### Documents to Keep
- [ ] Import permit (per shipment)
- [ ] Commercial invoices
- [ ] Packing lists
- [ ] Bills of lading
- [ ] Customs declaration receipts

### Important Notes
- Always declare correct value — under-declaration is illegal
- Keep records for 5 years (IRAS requirement)
- If using freight forwarder, they handle most of the process
- For shipments <$400, no import permit needed

## 8. HDB Home-Based Business Declaration

### What It Is
Singapore allows small-scale home-based businesses in HDB flats without additional license.

### Conditions (Must Comply)
| Condition | What It Means |
|-----------|---------------|
| No external employees | Cannot hire staff to work at home |
| No nuisance to neighbours | No excessive noise, traffic, or smell |
| No signage displayed | Cannot put business signs on HDB flat |
| No alteration to flat structure | Cannot modify flat for business use |
| Business activities only in flat | Cannot use common areas for business |

### What You Can Do
- Store inventory at home
- Pack and ship orders from home
- Manage online store from home
- Receive deliveries at home

### What You Cannot Do
- Have customers visit for pickup
- Hire staff to work at your flat
- Put up business signage
- Store excessive inventory (fire hazard)

### Step-by-Step Procedure

**Step 1: Review HDB Guidelines**
- Go to [https://www.hdb.gov.sg/residential/living-in-an-hdb-flat/rules-and-conditions](https://www.hdb.gov.sg/residential/living-in-an-hdb-flat/rules-and-conditions)
- Review "Home-Based Business" section

**Step 2: Ensure Compliance**
- Confirm no external employees
- Confirm no signage
- Confirm no structural alterations
- Confirm inventory storage is safe (not blocking exits)

**Step 3: No Formal Application Needed**
- **No license required** for small-scale home-based business
- No fee to pay
- No approval to wait for

**Step 4: Keep Records**
- Keep a declaration signed by yourself stating compliance with HDB rules
- Date and sign it
- Keep with business records

### Sample Declaration

```
HOME-BASED BUSINESS DECLARATION

I, [Name], FIN [Number], declare that:

1. I operate a small-scale home-based business at [HDB Address]
2. The business involves online retail of puzzles and toys
3. I comply with all HDB conditions for home-based businesses:
   - No external employees are hired to work at the premises
   - No nuisance is caused to neighbours
   - No signage is displayed at the premises
   - No alterations have been made to the flat structure
   - Business activities are conducted only within the flat

Signed: _________________
Date: ___________________
```

### Documents to Keep
- [ ] HDB Home-Based Business Guidelines (printed)
- [ ] Self-declaration (signed)
- [ ] Compliance checklist

### Important Notes
- HDB may conduct random checks
- If neighbours complain, HDB may investigate
- Violation may result in fine or requirement to cease business
- If business grows significantly, consider renting a small warehouse
